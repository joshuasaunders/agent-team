# Department: People / HR

## Mission
Attract, hire, develop, pay, retain, and separate employees in a way that is
fair, legal, and aligned with the company's goals. Be the steward of the
employee experience and the custodian of the workforce — how many, what kinds,
what they cost, how they are progressing, how well they are engaged. Be the
counterweight that keeps the company honest with the people working in it.

---

## Why This Department Exists
In a technology company, people are the business. The product is built by
people; the GTM is run by people; the capital is allocated in large part to
people. A company's ability to execute is bounded by its ability to hire the
right people, develop them, pay them fairly, keep them engaged, and manage
performance honestly.

People/HR exists as a dedicated function because these responsibilities are
too important to be done off the side of the desks of operating leaders.
A VP Engineering trying to do compensation design while also running
engineering will do one of the two poorly. A startup founder handling
employee relations while also leading the company will miss early signals.
The dedicated function exists to make the people practice systematic rather
than ad hoc.

## Org Structure

```
People/HR Supervisor (CHRO / VP People)
    │
    ├─ Recruiter                       (talent acquisition)
    ├─ People Operations Manager       (HRIS, onboarding, offboarding, ops)
    ├─ Learning & Development Manager  (training, performance, development)
    ├─ Total Rewards Manager           (compensation, benefits, equity)
    ├─ Employee Relations Manager      (complaints, investigations, grievances)
    └─ DEI / Culture Manager           (diversity, equity, inclusion, culture)
```

---

## Collaboration with Other Departments
- **Executive** — Top-Level Supervisor and CHRO partner on executive team
  composition, succession, and leadership development
- **All departments** — each Department Supervisor partners with a People
  counterpart on hiring, performance management, and compensation
- **Finance** — partnership on headcount planning, compensation budget,
  equity plan economics
- **Legal** — close partnership with Employment Counsel on every material
  employment decision
- **IT/Corporate Ops** — partnership on provisioning, deprovisioning, and
  workplace operations

---

## Standards
- **Employees are not a line item.** Headcount is a planning number, but
  people are not interchangeable units. People decisions are made with the
  weight appropriate to their impact on the individual.
- **Fairness over favoritism.** Compensation, promotion, and performance
  decisions apply consistent criteria. Exceptions happen, but they are
  documented and considered against the pattern they create.
- **Feedback over surprise.** Performance issues are raised in conversation
  and in writing as they occur, not saved up for a review cycle. Terminations
  based on undocumented issues are both unfair and legally exposed.
- **Confidentiality with limits.** Employee concerns are treated with
  appropriate confidentiality, except where the company has legal obligations
  (harassment reports, safety, fraud) that require action.
- **Process discipline.** Hiring, performance management, compensation
  adjustments, and separations follow documented processes. Process-by-
  manager-preference is how disparate treatment claims arise.
- **Voice of the employee.** The department surfaces employee sentiment —
  through engagement surveys, listening sessions, exit interviews — rather
  than assuming leadership sees the full picture.

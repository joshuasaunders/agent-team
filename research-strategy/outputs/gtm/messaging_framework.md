# Messaging Framework: Jewelry Insurance

## Segment 1: Mid-Market Fine Jewelry Owners (Primary ICP)

### Headline Message
**"Protect your jewelry with insurance you can afford and trust. Simple coverage, honest pricing, claims handled in days—not months."**

*Rationale:* Directly addresses primary pain points: affordability, trust in claims, simplicity. Contrasts with Jewelers Mutual (slower) and BriteCo (unreliable claims). Appeals to decision-driver hierarchy: coverage (protection) + price (afford) + reliability (trust).

### Supporting Proof Points

**Proof Point 1: Competitive Pricing (Price Pillar)**
- "1.25% annual premium — 35% less than traditional insurers like Jewelers Mutual"
- "A $2,500 necklace costs just $31/year to insure — less than $3/month"
- *Evidence:* Compare pricing table (Jewelers Mutual 1-2% = $31-50/year vs. ours 1.25% = $31/year)
- *Usage:* SEM ads, comparison tools, website pricing transparency

**Proof Point 2: Full Coverage (Coverage Pillar)**
- "100% of appraised value coverage, unlimited items, worldwide protection"
- "No caps per item, no limits on total collection value — we cover your entire collection"
- *Evidence:* Policy documentation showing unlimited coverage (vs. homeowners $1,500 cap)
- *Usage:* Website product details, quote tool, policy docs

**Proof Point 3: Fast Claims Resolution (Trust Pillar)**
- "95% of claims resolved in under 10 days — not 30+ days"
- "24-hour initial response from a real person — not a bot"
- *Evidence:* Published claims satisfaction score (NPS ≥55), customer testimonials ("My claim was settled in 8 days")
- *Usage:* Website testimonials, ads, comparison tools

**Proof Point 4: Transparent Underwriting (Trust Pillar)**
- "No surprise denials or hidden exclusions — we show you exactly what's covered"
- "Simple loss, theft, damage coverage — that's it. No confusing riders."
- *Evidence:* Simple policy document (1-2 pages vs. Jewelers Mutual complexity), FAQ addressing common questions
- *Usage:* Website, educational content, sales materials

**Proof Point 5: Digital Simplicity (Convenience Pillar)**
- "Quote in 60 seconds, activate coverage in under 2 minutes"
- "Manage your policy entirely online — view coverage, add items, file claims via app"
- *Evidence:* Product walkthrough video, website demo
- *Usage:* Website, SEM ads, video testimonials

### Competitive Contrast

**vs. Jewelers Mutual (Incumbent, High Trust, Higher Price, Slower)**
- **Our Advantage:** "Same trust in claims, 35% lower price + faster underwriting"
- **Messaging Angle:** "Jewelers Mutual is the safe choice if you don't mind paying 35% more. We offer the same coverage and claims reliability for less."
- **Key Comparison:** Price + speed (both of which matter to mid-market buyers)
- **When to Use:** SEM ads ("jewelry insurance"), price comparison tools, direct sales

**vs. BriteCo (InsurTech, Cheap, Modern UX, Inconsistent Claims)**
- **Our Advantage:** "Better pricing than traditional insurers without the claims headaches"
- **Messaging Angle:** "BriteCo is fast and cheap, but read the reviews — their claims can drag on for months. We're competitive on price without the risk."
- **Key Comparison:** Claims reliability + simplicity (not just price)
- **When to Use:** Reddit/forum conversations, customer comparison research, sales support

**vs. Zillion (Embedded POS, Frictionless, Limited Distribution)**
- **Our Advantage:** "Embedded insurance when you buy jewelry, PLUS the ability to insure your whole collection"
- **Messaging Angle:** "Zillion is great if your jeweler is a partner. But what if you shop elsewhere? We cover jewelry from any retailer."
- **Key Comparison:** Distribution flexibility + completeness
- **When to Use:** D2C marketing, jeweler partnerships, ads

**vs. Traditional Homeowners Add-on (Cheap, Convenient, Insufficient Coverage)**
- **Our Advantage:** "Homeowners insurance caps jewelry at $1,500. Your jewelry is worth more. Our coverage protects your full collection."
- **Messaging Angle:** "Your homeowners insurance isn't designed for fine jewelry. It'll cap you at $1,500 — that's not enough. We're designed specifically for jewelry protection."
- **Key Comparison:** Coverage completeness
- **When to Use:** Educational content, homeowners insurance comparison, insurance agent training

## Segment 2: Luxury Watch Collectors (Secondary ICP)

**"Comprehensive protection for your watch collection — specialized coverage, unlimited limits, concierge claims support."**

*Rationale:* Emphasizes specialization (not generic jewelry insurance), expertise, and high-service model. Watch collectors want to know they're dealing with someone who understands their collection.

### Supporting Proof Points

**Proof Point 1: Specialized Watch Expertise**
- "Written by collectors, for collectors — we understand Rolex, Patek Philippe, Audemars Piguet"
- "Authentication support included — we work with horological experts for rare pieces"
- *Evidence:* Team background (includes watch enthusiast), featured in watch forums/communities
- *Usage:* Horology community forums, collector websites, insurance broker materials

**Proof Point 2: Unlimited Coverage**
- "No caps per watch or per collection — cover your full collection value with no surprises"
- "Example: $500K watch collection — fully covered with zero limits"
- *Evidence:* Policy document showing unlimited per-item coverage
- *Usage:* Website product details, broker proposals

**Proof Point 3: Concierge Claims Service**
- "Dedicated watch claims specialist — calls you within 2 hours of claim filing"
- "Coordination with authorized service centers — your Rolex gets serviced by Rolex"
- *Evidence:* Customer testimonials, service level agreement documentation
- *Usage:* Website, broker proposals, sales presentations

**Proof Point 4: Travel & Mysterious Disappearance Coverage**
- "Covers mysterious disappearance — if your watch vanishes, we'll pay"
- "Worldwide coverage — travel to watch conventions and shows with full protection"
- *Evidence:* Policy language, exclusions list (what's NOT covered, emphasizing what IS covered)

**vs. Jewelers Mutual (Specialization + Modern Service)**

**vs. Chubb (Premium Without the Price)**

### Messaging Angles

**Angle 1: Horology Communities & Forums**
- Tone: Peer-to-peer, expert

- Usage: Reddit watchmaking forums, horology groups, watch show sponsorships

**Angle 2: Luxury Watch Retailers / Authorized Dealers**
- Tone: Professional, credible
- Message: "Offer your customers expert watch insurance as a value-add — we handle everything"
- Usage: Retailer partnerships, dealer training, point-of-sale materials

**Angle 3: Wealth Management Advisors / High-Net-Worth Brokers**

## Core Messaging Pillars (All Segments, Unified Brand)

### Pillar 1: Claims Excellence (Primary Differentiator)
**Internal Message:** "Exceptional claims execution is our competitive advantage; it's how we win retention and word-of-mouth"

**Customer-Facing Message:** "We handle claims the way you'd want them handled — transparently, fairly, fast"

**Why It Matters:** InsurTech disruptors focus on quote speed; we focus on claims reliability. This is where we differentiate and win long-term customer value.

**Evidence:**
- 95%+ claims satisfaction
- <10-day average claims resolution
- Customer testimonials emphasizing reliability
- Published NPS and satisfaction metrics

### Pillar 2: Affordable Protection (Secondary Differentiator)
**Internal Message:** "Competitive pricing enables market share gain; pricing at industry-low end makes us aggressive without being unsustainable"

**Customer-Facing Message:** "Full-value jewelry protection shouldn't cost a fortune"

**Why It Matters:** Mid-market buyers are price-sensitive; BriteCo proves demand is price-elastic. We undercut traditional carriers but maintain quality.

**Evidence:**
- 1.0-1.5% pricing comparison to competitors
- Pricing transparency (published rates, no "call for quote")
- Customer testimonials on value

### Pillar 3: Simplicity & Transparency (Supporting Differentiator)
**Internal Message:** "Simple product reduces operational complexity and customer confusion; transparency builds trust"

**Customer-Facing Message:** "Simple, honest insurance — no surprise exclusions, no confusing jargon"

**Why It Matters:** Traditional insurance is complex; digital disruption should mean simplicity. We deliver straightforward coverage without hidden limitations.

**Evidence:**
- Simple policy documents (2-3 pages vs. traditional 20+ pages)
- Transparent exclusions list
- Published FAQs addressing common questions
- Customer testimonials on simplicity

## Key Messaging Guidelines

### Tone & Voice
- **Professional but approachable** — use plain language, avoid insurance jargon
- **Confident but not arrogant** — we're solving a real problem, not reinventing insurance
- **Values-driven** — emphasize reliability and honesty, not hype

### What to Emphasize
- ✅ Claims reliability and transparency (primary)
- ✅ Competitive pricing (secondary)
- ✅ Simple, straightforward coverage (supporting)
- ✅ Customer testimonials and satisfaction metrics
- ✅ Specific comparisons to competitors (where we win)

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Hype or unsubstantiated claims ("best insurance ever")
- ❌ Technical insurance jargon (use plain language)
- ❌ Negative competitor attacks (let our positioning speak)
- ❌ Overpromising on claims turnaround (say "10 days"; deliver in 7)

### Messaging by Channel
- **Retail POS:** Ultra-simple (one-liner), transactional
- **SEM/Paid:** Direct, comparative, action-oriented
- **Owned Media:** Longer-form, storytelling, values-driven
- **Sales/Broker:** Professional, evidence-heavy, ROI-focused
- **Community Forums:** Peer-to-peer, authentic, expert

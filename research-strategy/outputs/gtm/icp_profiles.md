# Ideal Customer Profiles: Jewelry Insurance

## ICP 1: Mid-Market Fine Jewelry Owner (Primary Target)

### Firmographics

**Demographics:**
- **Age Range:** 30-60 years old
- **Gender:** Balanced (not male/female skewed)
- **Household Income:** $60,000-$150,000
- **Marital Status:** Mix of married (60%), single (25%), partnered (15%)
- **Children:** Often 1-3 children; empty-nesters also present

**Geographic Profile:**
- Suburban and urban areas, concentrated in metros with 100K+ population
- US-focused (all 50 states, highest concentration in CA, TX, NY, FL, IL, PA)
- Affluent neighborhoods in mid-sized to large metros

**Jewelry Profile:**
- **Total Collection Value:** $5,000-$25,000
- **Number of Pieces:** 5-12 pieces of fine jewelry
- **Value Per Item:** $1,000-$5,000
- **Item Types:** Mix of engagement rings, anniversary gifts, everyday fine jewelry (necklaces, earrings), some inherited/heirloom pieces
- **Jewelry Composition:** Mix of gold, diamonds, gemstones; mostly traditional metals (not costume/fashion jewelry)

### Buyer Persona: "Jennifer, 38, Suburban Jewelry Owner"

**Professional Profile:**
- Mid-level professional: manager, teacher, accountant, nurse, or business owner
- 10-15 years in current career
- Annual income: $85,000-$130,000 (household income $110,000-$160,000 with spouse)
- Works full-time, values work-life balance

**Personal Profile:**
- Married (13 years), 2 kids (ages 8 and 11)
- Purchased engagement ring ($5,000) at age 27
- Received anniversary gift ($2,500 diamond necklace) for 10-year anniversary
- Recently (last 2 years) purchased occasional gifts for herself ($1,000-$3,000)
- Family inheritance: received $2,000 grandmother's ring 3 years ago
- Total jewelry collection: approximately $12,000

**Lifestyle & Values:**
- Values family and experiences over material goods (but cares about meaningful jewelry)
- Conscious about financial stability and protecting assets
- Digitally comfortable (uses apps, online banking, ecommerce) but not tech-savvy
- Cautious decision-maker; researches major purchases
- Concerned about security (home alarm, car insurance, etc.)

**Jewelry Relationship:**
- Jewelry is emotionally significant (engagements, milestones, heirlooms) not status-seeking
- Wears jewelry daily (engagement ring, necklace)
- Worried about loss/damage ("What if my necklace breaks while traveling?")
- Recently had close call when earring clasp broke on plane; made her realize value of good insurance

### Critical Problems & Current Solutions

**Key Problems (In Priority Order):**

1. **Insufficient Homeowners Coverage**
   - Standard homeowners policy caps jewelry at $1,500-$5,000
   - Jennifer's collection ($12K) exceeds this significantly
   - Can't file claim for individual $2,500 necklace without hitting cap
   - Realizes this after reading policy post-purchase

2. **Insurance Product Fragmentation & Discovery Friction**
   - Doesn't know where to buy standalone jewelry insurance
   - Heard of Jewelers Mutual only through jeweler at purchase time
   - Never saw comparison options (BriteCo, Zillion, others)
   - Would use Google/comparison site if she knew to look

3. **Price Uncertainty & Hidden Costs**
   - Doesn't understand if 1% annual premium is expensive or cheap
   - Worried there are "surprise exclusions" or claim denials
   - Traditional insurers' complex policy language creates distrust

4. **Claims Process Anxiety**
   - Worried claims process will be bureaucratic and slow (homeowners experience: 30-45 days)
   - Fears she won't get full value; worried about appraisals and documentation
   - Has never filed jewelry claim; uncertain about process

5. **Time & Convenience Friction**
   - Can't easily add jewelry insurance to homeowners online; requires phone call
   - Busy with work and kids; doesn't want lengthy application process
   - Would prefer to handle online/mobile

**Current Solutions & Workarounds:**
- **Status quo:** Relies on homeowners insurance (insufficient but convenient)
- **Self-insurance:** Keeps savings account for jewelry replacement (uncomfortable with risk exposure)
- **Limited coverage:** Some jewelry uninsured (recently purchased pieces)
- **Retailer recommendation:** Bought Jewelers Mutual when purchasing anniversary gift (only because jeweler recommended it; didn't shop around)

### Purchase Criteria & Decision Drivers (Priority Order)

**1. Coverage Completeness** (Most Important)
- "Does this insurance cover the full value of my collection?"
- Wants clear limit statements: "Covers up to $25,000 per household" or "Unlimited per item"
- Needs explicit confirmation: loss, theft, damage, travel
- Not interested in add-ons (warranties, repair services); wants simple base coverage

**2. Affordability & Price Transparency** (Critical)
- "How much will this cost?" — wants clear, simple pricing
- Accepts market rate (~1.5% annually) but seeks 10-20% discount if available
- Willing to switch carriers for 20% savings (price-sensitive but not price-only buyer)
- No patience for "call for quote" opaque pricing

**3. Claims Certainty & Speed** (Critical but Secondary to Coverage)
- "If I file a claim, will I actually get paid?" — trust in claims is highest concern after coverage
- Wants clear claims process: "File photo, submit documentation, decision in 10 days"
- Values transparency: would pay 10% more for insurer with published 95%+ claims satisfaction
- Wants fast turnaround: <2 weeks, not 30+ days

**4. Ease of Purchase & Management** (Important but Not Deal-Breaker)
- Prefers online quote (<5 min) and instant activation
- Wants digital policy management (view coverage, download documents, file claims via app)
- Willing to do phone application if necessary but prefers self-serve
- Monthly payment option more appealing than annual (cash flow preference)

**5. Brand Credibility & Trust** (Supporting Factor)
- Doesn't need household name (not driven by Allstate/Liberty recognition)
- Wants credible signs: "Rated 4.5+ stars by customers," "Backed by major insurer," "110+ years in business"
- Wary of unknown online-only startups (concerned about legitimacy)
- Would trust BriteCo or Zillion if she'd heard of them (but hasn't)

### Accessibility & Reach (How to Reach Jennifer)

**Primary Channels (Most Effective):**

1. **Retail Point-of-Sale at Jewelry Purchase** (#1 Highest Intent)
   - Jennifer is most receptive to insurance pitch at moment of jewelry purchase
   - "Add insurance for just $X/month?" at checkout is highest conversion
   - Requires retail partnerships with jewelry stores where Jennifer shops

2. **Online Search** (Google) (#2 High Intent)
   - After having a close call (broken earring, loss scare) or reading homeowners policy, Jennifer searches "jewelry insurance"
   - High-intent search; willing to compare quotes and switch
   - Targets: SEM on "jewelry insurance," "engagement ring insurance," "jewelry protection"

3. **Jeweler Recommendation During Purchase** (#3 Moderate Intent)
   - If her jeweler proactively recommends insurance (like at diamond store)
   - Trust in jeweler leads to trust in recommended insurer
   - Requires jeweler partnerships and staff training

**Secondary Channels:**

4. **Insurance Agent / Homeowners Insurance Renewal** (Lower Intent)
   - When renewing auto/homeowners insurance, may ask about jewelry coverage
   - Agent recommendation carries weight; but limited reach without agent partnerships

5. **Word-of-Mouth / Referral** (High Trust but Low Volume)
   - If friend or family member had positive claims experience, Jennifer would listen
   - Not a primary acquisition channel but high retention/referral value

6. **Social Media / Influencer** (Very Low for This ICP)
   - Jennifer not heavily influenced by Instagram/Facebook advertising
   - More skeptical of digital ads than younger demographics
   - Not recommended as primary channel for this segment

## ICP 2: Luxury Watch Collector (Secondary Target)

### Firmographics

**Demographics:**
- **Age Range:** 35-70 years old
- **Gender:** Male-dominant (70-75% male; growing female segment)
- **Household Income:** $150,000+
- **Geographic Concentration:** Major metropolitan areas; highest in NYC, LA, San Francisco, Chicago

**Watch Collection Profile:**
- **Collection Value:** $50,000-$500,000+
- **Number of Watches:** 3-20+ watches (serious collectors have 5-15+)
- **Value Per Watch:** $5,000-$100,000+ (luxury brands)
- **Watch Types:** Mix of Rolex, Patek Philippe, Audemars Piguet, Omega, other luxury brands
- **Purchase Pattern:** Mix of self-purchases ($5K-$20K per watch) and investment purchases

**Buyer Motivation:**
- Investment-minded (watches hold or appreciate value)
- Brand enthusiast (passionate about watchmaking and horology)
- Status-conscious (visible wealth marker)
- Collector mindset (enjoys curating collection)

### Buyer Persona: "Michael, 52, Luxury Watch Collector"

**Professional Profile:**
- Senior executive or successful entrepreneur
- Annual income: $200,000-$500,000+
- Established career, confident in professional decisions

**Personal Profile:**
- High net worth ($1M-$5M+ net worth)
- Married, no young children
- Sophisticated buyer; makes data-driven decisions
- Engaged with horology community (watch forums, auctions, collector groups)

**Watch Collection:**
- Collection value: $150,000
- Key pieces: Rolex Submariner ($8K), Patek Philippe Nautilus ($35K), Audemars Piguet Royal Oak ($25K), etc.
- Recently purchased luxury sport watch ($40K)
- Attends watch auctions, collector meetups

**Ownership Concerns:**
- Concerned about theft during travel (conventions, shows)
- Concerned about damage (scratches, damage during daily wear)
- Worried about authentication/appraisals for high-value pieces
- Concerned about jewelry homeowners caps (insufficient for collection value)

### Critical Problems

**Problem 1: Standard Homeowners Insurance Insufficient**
- Homeowners policy caps jewelry at $1,500-$5,000
- Michael's collection ($150K) far exceeds this
- Requires separate specialty insurance

**Problem 2: Specialized Underwriting & Expertise Required**
- High-value watches require authentication, appraisals, specialist knowledge
- Generic jewelry insurance may not provide adequate documentation
- Needs underwriter who understands Rolex/Patek Philippe/AP collector market

**Problem 3: International Coverage & Travel Risks**
- Michael travels with watches to conventions, shows, meets with other collectors
- Needs explicit worldwide coverage, mysterious disappearance protection
- Concerned about coverage gaps outside US

**Problem 4: Replacement/Repair Complexity**
- If Rolex is damaged, wants it repaired by Rolex service (not generic jeweler)
- If Patek Philippe is lost, wants specialized search/recovery
- Standard "replacement value" insufficient for unique/rare pieces

### Purchase Criteria

**Top Priority: Full Coverage Without Caps**
- Wants unlimited coverage per watch or very high limits ($100K+)
- Zero deductible preferred
- No exclusions for mysterious disappearance or travel

**Secondary: Specialized Expertise**
- Wants underwriter who understands luxury watches
- Values authentication support and appraisal collaboration
- Wants claims adjuster who can handle specialized damage assessment

**Tertiary: Claims Speed & Hassle-Free Experience**
- Willing to pay premium for superior claims experience
- Values concierge support for claims
- Wants transparent, documented process

### Accessibility & Reach

**Primary Channels:**
1. **Horology/Collector Communities** (Forums, clubs, shows)
2. **Luxury Jeweler Recommendations**
3. **Specialized Insurance Brokers** (focusing on high-net-worth)
4. **Online Search** (luxury watch insurance, Rolex insurance)

**Secondary Channels:**
5. **Wealth Management / Private Banking** (financial advisor recommendations)
6. **Luxury Retail Partners** (watch boutiques, authorized dealers)

## ICP 3: Ethical/Sustainable Jewelry Buyer (Tertiary Opportunity)

### Firmographics

**Demographics:**
- **Age Range:** 25-45 years old (Millennials and Gen Z focus)
- **Gender:** Balanced or slightly female-skewed (women more engaged with sustainable purchasing)
- **Household Income:** $75,000-$150,000
- **Values:** Environmental consciousness, ethical sourcing, ESG alignment

**Jewelry Profile:**
- **Jewelry Type:** Lab-grown diamonds, ethically sourced gemstones, sustainable brands
- **Brands:** Brilliant Earth, Forevermark Lab, James Allen, Warerly (sustainable brands)
- **Collection Value:** $3,000-$20,000
- **Purchase Pattern:** Intentional, values-driven; often single high-value purchase (lab-grown engagement ring)

### Buyer Persona: "Sarah, 31, Sustainable Jewelry Advocate"

**Personal Profile:**
- Marketing professional, values-driven career choices
- Engaged (purchased lab-grown diamond engagement ring from Brilliant Earth)
- Active in sustainability/ESG communities
- Willing to pay 10-20% premium for sustainable alternatives

**Values Alignment:**
- Avoids conflict diamonds; concerned about mining environmental impact
- Attracted to lab-grown diamonds (identical physical properties, created in lab, no mining)
- Aligns purchasing with personal values
- Wants insurance provider who shares values

**Jewelry Needs:**
- Wants to insure engagement ring ($5,000 lab-grown diamond)
- Wants assurance that insurance provider supports sustainable practices
- Open to sustainability-focused insurance products (carbon offset, etc.)

### Critical Problems

**Problem 1: Limited Insurance Options for Lab-Grown Jewelry**
- Doesn't know if mainstream insurers cover lab-grown diamonds
- Worried about undervaluation of lab-grown vs. mined diamonds
- Wants explicit confirmation: "Lab-grown diamonds covered at full replacement value"

**Problem 2: Lack of Values Alignment in Insurance**
- Wants insurance company that shares sustainability values
- Frustrated if insurer doesn't support ethical practices
- Attracted to partnerships between ethical jewelers (Brilliant Earth) and insurers

### Purchase Criteria

**Primary: Values Alignment**
- Wants insurance partner that supports sustainability
- Attracted to ESG-focused messaging
- Willing to pay premium for aligned insurer

**Secondary: Clear Lab-Grown Coverage**
- Wants explicit confirmation lab-grown diamonds fully covered
- Wants no differentiation in pricing between mined and lab-grown diamonds
- Values transparency on this topic

**Tertiary: Competitive Pricing**
- Price-sensitive but not price-only decision-maker
- Willing to pay 5-10% premium for values alignment

### Accessibility & Reach

**Primary Channels:**
1. **Ethical Jewelry Brand Partnerships** (Brilliant Earth, Forevermark Lab)
2. **Millennial-Focused Marketing** (Instagram, sustainability content)
3. **Environmental/ESG Communities**

**Secondary Channels:**
4. **Online Search** (sustainable jewelry insurance, ethical diamond insurance)
5. **Wedding Planning Content** (sustainable wedding guides)

## Summary: ICP Selection Rationale

**Primary ICP: Mid-Market Fine Jewelry Owner**
- **Market Size:** 60M US households (largest addressable market)
- **Penetration:** 34% (most underpenetrated; highest growth opportunity)
- **Accessibility:** Reachable via retail partnerships and D2C digital
- **Unit Economics:** $30-50 average annual premium per customer; 75% retention achievable
- **Competitive Intensity:** Lowest — no single dominant competitor; Jewelers Mutual focuses on engagement rings; BriteCo focuses on millennials

**Secondary ICP: Luxury Watch Collector**
- **Market Size:** 5-10M serious collectors (smaller but high-value)
- **Profit Margin:** 2-3x higher premium per customer ($100K collection × 1.5% = $1,500/year)
- **Growth Trend:** Watch insurance growing 20%+ CAGR (emerging segment)
- **Accessibility:** Specialized distribution (collector communities, brokers)
- **Competitive Risk:** BriteCo already strong in watches; positioned as secondary target for future expansion once primary segment scales

**Tertiary ICP: Sustainable Jewelry Buyer**
- **Market Size:** Emerging segment; estimated <5% of market currently but growing
- **Growth Trend:** 70% of millennials prefer sustainable jewelry (high future potential)
- **Strategic Value:** Differentiates from competitors; builds brand affinity with values-driven customers
- **Accessibility:** Through ethical jewelry brand partnerships (Brilliant Earth model)
- **Timing:** Future consideration; address after primary segment dominance established

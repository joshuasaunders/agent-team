# Executive Summary: Jewelry Insurance Market Strategy

## Strategic Situation

The jewelry insurance market ($2.25B, 6-7% CAGR) bifurcates into specialty incumbents and digital disruptors with no clear market consolidation. The commissioning company can win by specializing in the mid-market jewelry segment (60M households at 34% penetration, no dominant competitor), capturing customers at retail point-of-sale (fastest-growing channel, 12-18 month window before consolidation), and differentiating on claims excellence (the critical weakness of InsurTech competitors). This strategy avoids head-to-head direct-to-consumer competition with better-funded BriteCo while building defensible moats through retail partnerships and claims reputation.

## Top 3 Recommendations

1. **Lead with Retail Point-of-Sale Partnerships (Primary Channel)**
   Invest 60% of resources into signing exclusive or preferred partnerships with independent jewelers and mid-market chains (Helzberg, Shane Co., regional boutiques). Develop turn-key POS integration enabling 1-click insurance at checkout. Target 5-10 exclusive partnerships by month 6, 30-50 by month 18. This captures the fastest-growing channel (retail POS growing faster than DTC), achieves lowest CAC ($40-50 vs. $100-150 SEM), and creates defensible moats before consolidation locks up retailers.

2. **Differentiate on Claims Excellence (Core Positioning)**
   Build claims operations as core competitive advantage, targeting <10-day resolution and 95%+ claims satisfaction. Develop concierge-level support (on-site assessment, transparent decision-making, specialized restoration partnerships). Position as "Competitive pricing with claims you can trust" (1.5% baseline vs. Jewelers Mutual's 1-2%, underpricing BriteCo's potential unsustainability). This directly addresses the #1 hidden weakness of InsurTech competitors and drives retention + word-of-mouth.

3. **Specialize Exclusively in Mid-Market Fine Jewelry ($1K-$5K Items)**
   Don't attempt broad market coverage. Target age 30-60, $60K-$150K household income, mid-tier fine jewelry owners. Build product, distribution, and messaging specifically for this segment (no engagement ring focus, no ultra-HNW complexity). This follows the proven pattern in the market—no competitor has dominated this segment; specialization outperforms generalism. Reserve luxury watches and ethical jewelry for year 2+ expansion.

## Most Important First Action

**By Month 1: Hire Dedicated Retail Partnership Lead and Engage Insurance Licensing Attorney**

These two actions unlock the core strategy. The retail partnership lead identifies and begins relationship-building with independent jewelry retailers and mid-market chains (Helzberg franchisees, Shane Co. franchisees, regional boutiques) before consolidation accelerates. The insurance attorney immediately files multi-state licensing applications (start with 5-10 largest states: CA, NY, TX, FL, IL, PA, OH, NC, MI, VA). Licensing is a critical path item—it determines launch speed. Parallel licensing + partnership development means you're ready to execute both channels simultaneously in month 6. Delaying either one cascades into launch delays and missed market share. This is the single highest-leverage action: without retail partnerships, you compete head-to-head with BriteCo on DTC (losing); without licensing, you can't write policies even if partnerships are signed.

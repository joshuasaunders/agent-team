# Action Plan: Jewelry Insurance Market Strategy

## Near-Term Actions (0-30 days)

### Action 1: Hire Retail Partnership Lead & Engage Insurance Licensing Attorney (CRITICAL PATH)
- **Owner Role**: VP Business Development (if hiring external, interim Head of Partnerships)
- **Timeline**: Complete by Day 15
- **Dependencies**: None (start immediately)
- **Success Metric**: (a) Partnership lead identified, onboarded, relationship-building begun with 10+ retailers by day 20; (b) Insurance attorney engaged, licensing strategy for 10 target states documented, applications initiated
- **Context**: Licensing is serial path to launch; retail partnerships are parallel with claims ops build. Both must start simultaneously to hit month 6 goals.

### Action 2: Build Claims Operations Infrastructure Plan (CRITICAL PATH)
- **Owner Role**: VP Operations / Claims Lead
- **Timeline**: Complete by Day 30
- **Dependencies**: None
- **Success Metric**: Documented plan for claims operations, including (a) TPA partnership vs. in-house capability trade-off analysis, (b) target <10-day resolution processes mapped, (c) specialist network (restoration partners, appraisers) sourcing plan, (d) budget estimate ($500K-2M year 1), (e) pilot metro selection (recommend Chicago, NYC, LA for specialist density)
- **Context**: Claims is the core differentiator; must be planned in parallel with partnership building to stay on timeline.

---

## Medium-Term Actions (30-90 days)

### Action 3: File Multi-State Insurance Licensing Applications (5-10 States) (CRITICAL PATH)
- **Owner Role**: Legal/Compliance Lead
- **Timeline**: Applications filed by Day 60; expect 2-4 month approval cycles per state
- **Dependencies**: Action 1 (attorney engaged)
- **Success Metric**: (a) Applications filed in 5-10 largest states (CA, NY, TX, FL, IL, PA, OH, NC, MI, VA), (b) First approvals received by month 4, (c) 10+ states licensed by end of month 6
- **Context**: Each state takes 2-4 months post-filing. Filing in top 10 by day 60 gets you to month 4-6 first licenses. Launch should not wait for all 50 states—launch in 10 and expand.

### Action 4: Sign 3-5 Exclusive Retail Partnerships & Build POS Integration (CRITICAL PATH)
- **Owner Role**: Partnership Lead / Product Engineering
- **Timeline**: Agreements signed by Day 90; integration complete by Day 150
- **Dependencies**: Action 1 (partnership lead engaged); Action 2 (claims operations plan documented—partners will ask how claims work)
- **Success Metric**: (a) 3-5 exclusive or preferred-provider agreements signed with regional jewelry chains or independent jeweler groups, (b) Revenue-share terms documented (target 70/30 insurer/retailer split), (c) POS integration begun (Lightspeed, Shopify, custom APIs), (d) Staff training curriculum drafted for partner retail staff
- **Context**: This is the core of the strategy. Quality of partnerships > quantity. Target independent jewelers and mid-market chains, not national chains (those may be locked up). Focus on retailers with 10-50 locations, still in growth phase.

### Action 5: Develop Product & Pricing Strategy (Finalize)
- **Owner Role**: Product Manager / Pricing Analytics
- **Timeline**: Complete by Day 75
- **Dependencies**: Retail partners confirmed (know what coverage/pricing they need); claims operations plan documented
- **Success Metric**: (a) Mid-market focused product spec (loss, theft, damage; no exotic riders; simple underwriting), (b) Pricing model at 1.0-1.5% baseline (adjust by item value/segment), (c) Coverage limits published (recommend no caps; match or beat BriteCo on coverage breadth), (d) Underwriting rules documented, (e) Dynamic risk scoring roadmap outlined (for month 9 build, not month 1 launch)
- **Context**: Product should be simple and focused—avoid feature bloat. Let claims excellence be the differentiator, not product complexity.

## Longer-Term Actions (90+ days)

### Action 6: Launch Direct-to-Consumer Website & SEM Campaigns (Supporting Channel)
- **Owner Role**: Marketing Lead / Digital Manager
- **Timeline**: Website live by Day 120; SEM campaigns by Day 150
- **Dependencies**: Product finalized (Action 5); Claims operations operational (Action 2); Licensing in 5+ states (Action 3)
- **Success Metric**: (a) Website launched with quote, bind, policy management capabilities, (b) Claims messaging (concierge support, <10-day resolution, 95%+ satisfaction) prominently featured, (c) SEM campaigns live in 5-10 major metros (target mid-market jewelry owners, age 30-60, high HHI), (d) CAC target <$100 for DTC (secondary channel, so higher CAC acceptable vs. retail $40-50)
- **Context**: DTC is supporting channel (40% resource allocation). It enables customers to buy for out-of-network purchases and creates brand presence. Don't over-invest here; retail partnerships are primary growth lever.

### Action 7: Establish Claims Operations in 1-2 Pilot Metros (Build-Out)
- **Owner Role**: VP Operations / Claims Lead
- **Timeline**: Ops launched in 1-2 metros by Day 120; scale to 5 metros by month 18
- **Dependencies**: Action 2 (operations plan), TPA partnership or in-house hiring
- **Success Metric**: (a) Pilot metro (recommend Chicago or NYC) with specialist network recruited and trained (10-15 restoration partners, 3-5 appraisers), (b) Claims volume (target 500-1,000 policies in pilot metro by month 6), (c) Resolution time tracked weekly, targeting <10-day median, (d) Claims satisfaction surveyed monthly, targeting 95%+, (e) Customer NPS on claims >70
- **Context**: Claims is differentiator; pilot in 1-2 metros first, prove concept, then scale to 5 metros. Don't try to launch claims in all 50 states day 1—focus and iterate.

### Action 8: Expand Retail Partnerships to 30-50 Retailers (Scaling)
- **Owner Role**: Partnership Lead / Partnership Operations
- **Timeline**: Partnerships signed by month 12; 30-50 by month 18
- **Dependencies**: Action 4 (first 5 partnerships successful, case studies built); Action 3 (multi-state licensing achieved)
- **Success Metric**: (a) 30-50 exclusive or preferred partnerships signed, (b) Partner satisfaction tracked (NPS from partners target >60), (c) Policy volume from partners increasing MoM (target 500-1,000 policies/month by month 12), (d) Churn from retail channel baseline established (target 75%+), (e) CAC from retail partners <$50/policy
- **Context**: Growth comes from partner expansion, not adding more DTC SEM. Hire dedicated partnership operations team to support retailers. Train partners on insurance positioning, handle their customer escalations, provide co-marketing.

### Action 9: Build Dynamic Risk Scoring Model (Product Innovation)
- **Owner Role**: Data Science Lead / Analytics
- **Timeline**: Model built & A/B testing by month 9-10; rollout by month 12
- **Dependencies**: Action 1 (6+ months of customer claims data) 
- **Success Metric**: (a) Risk score model built using demographics, geography, storage, jewelry type, claim history, (b) A/B testing with new customer cohort shows 15%+ margin improvement vs. flat pricing, (c) Customer-facing risk score tool (web app showing how to lower score) launched, driving engagement, (d) Pricing impact analyzed, scores implemented as 1.0% (low-risk) to 1.5% (high-risk) range
- **Context**: This is emerging table stakes; competitors will follow. First-mover advantage in data-driven pricing is 12-18 month window before BriteCo/Jewelers Mutual copy.

### Action 10: Launch Quick-Win Innovation Channel—Influencer Affiliate Network (Adjacent Revenue)
- **Owner Role**: Marketing Lead / Growth Manager
- **Timeline**: Program live by month 9
- **Dependencies**: Product finalized (Action 5); website live (Action 6)
- **Success Metric**: (a) Affiliate program platform built (Refersion, ShareASale, or custom), (b) 20-30 jewelry influencers recruited (YouTube watch reviewers, Instagram designers, TikTok jewelry creators), (c) Commission structure live (7% per referral), (d) Content funding program live ($200-500 per creator per month for co-branded content), (e) Monthly referral volume tracked, target $50K+/month by month 12
- **Context**: Low-effort, high-ROI channel. Use to build brand awareness while retail partnerships and claims ops scale. Creators are eager for revenue share; no need for large upfront investment.

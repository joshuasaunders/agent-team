## Mission
Own the post-sale customer relationship. Drive successful onboarding, adoption,
retention, and expansion of existing customers. Be the voice of the customer
internally. Reduce churn by identifying and resolving risk early. Convert
successful customers into references, case studies, and sources of expansion
revenue.

---

## Why This Department Exists
In a software/technology company with a recurring revenue model, post-sale
customer outcomes are where the business case is either proven or lost. A deal
closed in Sales is not revenue retained — it is revenue at risk until the
customer has onboarded, adopted the product, achieved the outcome they bought
it for, and decided to renew and expand.

Customer Success sits between Sales (who handed off the relationship at close),
Product (whose roadmap shapes what customers can actually do), Support (who
handles reactive tickets), and Finance (who cares about retention metrics and
renewal dates). Without a dedicated function, each of those orgs optimizes for
its own metrics and the customer experience fractures at the seams.

---

## Intended Scope (to be detailed when agents are built)
When built out, this department will cover:

- **Customer onboarding** — from deal close through time-to-first-value, with
  defined milestones, measurable adoption criteria, and escalation paths when
  implementation stalls
- **Ongoing success management** — executive business reviews, success plans,
  outcome tracking, expansion identification
- **Customer support (tiered)** — reactive support for product issues, coordinated
  with Engineering for product-root-cause cases
- **Technical account management** — deep technical partnership for strategic
  accounts with complex implementations
- **Renewal management** — renewal forecasting, renewal negotiation, churn
  prevention
- **Customer education** — documentation, training, community, certification
  programs
- **Voice of the customer** — structured customer feedback to Product, Engineering,
  Sales, and Marketing
- **Customer references and advocacy** — case studies, references, community-led
  growth

Anticipated agent count: 6–7 specialists under the supervisor.

## Why This Is a Placeholder
- The first pass of the agent team is focused on the departments most
  immediately useful for business analysis, strategic research, product
  development, and go-to-market execution.
- Customer Success requires close coordination with Product, Support tooling,
  and CRM data that will be more fruitful to design once the first wave of
  departments has been used in real runs.
- Leaving this as a placeholder is preferable to half-building it — an
  under-specified Customer Success agent would produce advice that conflicts
  with Sales-at-close and Product-at-roadmap without the structure to resolve
  the conflict.

## Placeholder References
Until this department is built, the following placeholders apply:

- **Collaboration protocols** in other departments that mention Customer
  Success refer to this department's future scope as described above
- **Deal close handoffs** from Sales should be captured in a standardized
  handoff brief (defined by the Account Executive) and held pending the
  onboarding role to be authored
- **Product feedback loops** that would normally run through CSMs should
  currently be routed via the Product Researcher and the Product Supervisor
- **Renewal forecasts** that would normally originate here should currently
  be estimated by Sales Ops using existing-customer data and flagged as
  provisional
